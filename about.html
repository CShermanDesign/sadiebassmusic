Country singer/songwriter Sadie Bass brings a mix of small-town grit, outdoor roots, and modern edge to Nashville's country scene. Originally from Bath, Michigan, she first built a following through her work as an outdoorswoman on television, appearing on series across Discovery, Sportsman, and Outdoors channels.

After moving to Nashville to pursue music full-time, Bass gained national attention as a contestant on NBC's The Voice in 2022. She hit the ground running the following year, independently touring 80 shows across 20 states.

In 2024, Bass signed with WME and went on to perform major festivals including Tortuga, Watershed, CCMF, Barefoot, and Faster Horses. She also joined Kid Rock's Rock The Country festivals and provided direct support on tour for Randy Houser and Justin Moore.

In 2024, Bass signed a publishing deal with Sony Music Publishing, followed by a management deal with The Familie. After spending much of 2025 writing and recording, she is eager to hit the road again in 2026 and is set to release new music consistently throughout the year.

As an independent artist, Bass has built her career from the ground up—playing hundreds of shows across almost all 50 states, generating millions of streams, and growing a social audience of over 600,000 fans. With a brand rooted in authenticity, humor, and Midwest values, she continues carving out her own lane in country music.
